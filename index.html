About Me

My previous career as a professional chef of eight years was abruptly put on hold in early 2020 due to the situation we found ourselves in globally, out of necessity, I, like so many others had to pivot direction so I joined BRS1, Amazon’s large fulfilment centre in Bristol U.K.

Although I didn’t realise it at the time this was truly a blessing in disguise and has been a great opportunity to hone existing skills and develop many more.

Through hard work and determination, I have grown into a leadership position within the company, monitoring operations as an FC team lead overseeing a department of 70+ Amazon Associates.

However, by far the most positive thing to come from this change is that I have been able to make use of Amazon’s career choice, an amazing program designed to assist with employee tuition, granting the ability to continue working whilst also studying, gaining knowledge and technical skills in a wide range of sectors, in my case the exciting world of software development.

C1 Software Development - Month 3 - March

Starting of with week 9 we've been learning all things CMS (content management system) in particular WordPress, understanding posts, categories, tags, plugins, and much more. We've explored the amazing themes, plugins, and other resources available to build professional sites. We also went under the hood and altered the underlying HTML and CSS within WordPress to customize.

Week 10 & 11 have been the modules I've been looking forward to most, JavaScript! Variables, functions, conditional logic, loops, data types, and syntax, so much to learn and I'm really having a fun time with it!

C1 Software Development - Month 2 - February

Week 5 & 6 things have really started to progress which has been amazing, we've been looking into file structure and its importance, DNS (Domain Name System), web hosting, SEO (search engine optimization) and VCS (Version Control Systems).

Git and Github have also been a big focus, understanding the terminal for Git and for GitHub creating an account, creating a repository, creating a branch, committing changes, using pull requests and merging. The Coding hasn't stopped either, I have now created a full portfolio website and hosted it with Github pages.

In weeks 7 & 8 we've covered UX/UI, accessibility, and inclusive design in detail. Understanding mockups, being able to identify low, medium, and high fidelity mockups and differentiating between wireframes and prototypes. Using visual design tools such as Figma we have created our own wireframes, sitemaps and personas. We've also covered qualitative and quantitative data, user interviews and SEO.

On the Career front, I have created a LinkedIn account and I have started networking.

C1 Software Development - Month 1 - January

Week 1 & 2 of studying with correlation one's software development program has been great, so far we've deep-dived into HTML, learning whilst building basic web page projects inside the Codio IDE (Integrated development environment) and getting to grips with all different types of tags, syntax, best practices and much more!

We’ve also taken a look at the differences between the world wide web and the Internet, two terms that are often used interchangeably but are far in fact two different things.

Learning as a community has been amazing, and seeing how others choose to build and design their pages and share ideas.

Week 3 & 4 have been really interesting, we've jumped into all things CSS (Cascading Style Sheets) discussing the pros and cons of external, internal and inline styles. We have gone through the basics like selectors, declarations, properties and values right through to more complex ideas like box model, grids and flexbox.

Bootstrap and SCSS are two things we have also been learning this past fornite, all the while using these skills to build responsive and engaging projects.

Great feedback from my career coach Alan regarding my CV, he suggested some changes which were very insightful and I implemented them (mainly to showcase how skills are transferable).
